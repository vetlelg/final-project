Air pollution detection

The Expozance detects the presence and concentration of five of major pollutants; ground-level ozone, particle pollution, carbon monoxide, sulfur dioxide and nitrogen dioxide. These measurements are used to determine outdoor air quality. In addition, the Expozance also detects and measures the concentration of radon, asbestos, cigarette smoke and other substances to determine indoor air quality.

With these real-time measurements your bracelet will tell you when the air surrounding you is safe and when you should leave your current environment. This will be shown by different color-codes and air-quality-measurements. The air quality measurements will be done according to the Air Quality Index, which is the standard measurement of air pollution used across most of the world [5].

Virus update

As real-time virus detection is not yet possible, we still want to keep you updated during these times of the coronavirus. We understand the continuing stream of constantly changing information can be tedious, which makes the importance of getting updated information easily imperative. The device will use your location and get information about the risk of exposure from statistics provided by the Norwegian Institute of Public Health (NIPH) [6]. These statistics will be updated daily and shows number of reported cases in the county and municipality where you are located at the moment. Therefore, if you travel from county to county, municipality to municipality, you will be informed instantly and easily.

The device use the traffic light system [7], a system of three colors - red, yellow and green - representing risk levels:

  • Red zone - high risk
  • Yellow zone - moderate risk
  • Green zone - low risk

Bracelet function

The Expozance has some different functions to accomodate your preferences.

One of the choices is how you want to see the information. You can choose to display both air-pollution and virus information at the same time with a split screen function. Or you can have either as your default screen and use the button on the side to change screen.

In addition to color codes there will be text describing what zone you are in both for air-pollution and coronavirus. This is to make it easier sholud you suffer from color blindness.

To accommodate your preferences you can choose to have a sound or vibration played to alert you of zone-changes. Both from a lower danger level to a higher one and from a higher to a lower danger level. In addition there is a choice to substitute sound with a voice telling you what changed. This might especially be beneficial should you have trouble with your eyesight.

You can easily access the settings screen by holding the button on the side for about 3 seconds. Here you will have volume options, choices of voice, sound or vibration and split screen or two screens.
