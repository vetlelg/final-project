Air pollution

About seven million people die worldwide every year of air pollution. Ambient air pollution accounts for 4.2 million deaths each year, while exposure to smoke from cooking accounts for 3.8 million premature deaths yearly. In 2016 91% of the world population breathe air that contains high levels of pollutants according to WHO. Most of the deaths occur in low- and middle-income countries in south-east Asia and Western Pacific regions. Air pollution increases risk of mortality from heart disease, stroke, lung cancer and acute respiratory infections.
